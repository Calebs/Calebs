    Professional information

    Edit

    I am a cognitive neuroscientist investigating (mostly) decision making, learning and consciousness. Recently, I have been particularly interested in understanding how neuroscience and experimental psychology may help improve education.

    With my friend Mariano Sardon we have walked back and forth the path between art and science. Our shared work has traveled all over the world and was exhibited recently in the Puschkin Museum and in the wonderful Palazzo Fortuny in the 2017 Venice Biennale.

    I was twice at TED; in 2016 I gave a talk on how words can help us understand the past and the future of consciousness and on 2017, with Dan Ariely, we performed a massive experiment with all the crowd at TED.

    I wrote "The secret life of the mind" where I describe neuroscience in the way I see it; as a scientific voyage to understand the human condition and with this, hopefully, help us to better communicate.

    In 2016 I received in the Vatican the Pius XI medal by the Pontifical Academy of Sciences, very proud to share this prize with Stephen Hawking and many Nobel Laureates and scientists I admire so much.
